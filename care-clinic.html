Care Clinic

Facilitating Self Care and Reflection

Project Type

A solo project completed through the UX design Course from Career Foundry. The project was one of 3 to choose from and I chose this one due to my background in nursing and personal experience with mental health.

Objective

Allow health-conscious individuals to log in to a responsive health and wellbeing portal to record their health and medical information, and access general physical and mental wellbeing features.

Understanding The User

The Design Process

User Design is not an end-goal, but rather it is a long-term process and utilising the Double Diamond Strategy allows everyone to have a defined value structure with definitive points along the path so that all members of the multidisplinary team know where the project stands at any given time. To start Phase 1: Research (design the right thing), I needed a catalyst to ignite the thinking process. This being the problem statement, which encapsulates identifying our persona, problem and goals.

Mindfulness & Selfcare

Mental health problems such as anxiety, depression and stress have been increasing many countries due to event over recent years. Mindfulness-based interventions can be an evidence-based treatment for individuals with such struggles. However, mental health is such a personalised experience, possible problems can arise when designing an app to meet these needs.

Possible Problems

Consitancy: Mental health and its impact on an individuals mental, emotional and physical can significantly hinder one's ability to remain consistent

Consumer Education: Mental health is a broad and complex topic that can present differently person to person. And knowing where to start selfcare and mindfulness can be overwhelming

Design: The mental load of individuals accessing an app could already be quite heavy. To help reduce this mental load the design of the app needs be intuitive as possible

Problem Statement

Care Clinic is a responsive web app for individuals experiencing mental health, who need a way to facilitate informed self reflection and promote practicing selfcare strategies because they want to a holistic approach to their health.

Simple Journaling: complete mood journal in a minimal number of clicks.

Resources: access educational content for informed decision making.

Feedback: weekly and monthly statistics of mood and activities completed.

Customisation: like and save resources, add activities, change moods

Research

User Interviews

At the discovery phase of my project, I conducted user interviews in order to get a better understanding of the problem. Research points included:

  • what do users understand about mental health
  • what do users understand about selfcare
  • how are users completing selfcare
  • what apps are they already using
  • what obstacles are between the user and completing selfcare

Analysis

Users has some understanding about mental health through their own experiences, but wanted to expand their knowledge. Allocating time for self care was a large hurtle for many and core features wanted by the target audience included mood tracking, journaling and an educational tool.

Persona

Based on the interviews of 5 people, I set up a proto-persona. I referred to them throughout the entire product development process.

Having a deep understanding of the target audience is fundamental to creating a memorable and exceptional experience. Having a proto-persona helped me answer the important question of "who am I designing for?" By understanding the expectations, concerns and motivations of my target users, I can design an experience and product that will satisfy users' needs and therefore be successful.

User Journey

I mapped out the prospective users' steps with the insight I gained from my proto-persona to see how I could simplify their journey to help them reach their most important goals with the product.

I knew that the journal entry feature would be the most used core feature of Care Clinic so I wanted to quickly identify possible pain points in the flow of this feature. Additionally, creating a user journey map allowed me to identify the users' needs, possible interactions and emotions throughout their experience

Foundational Design

With the research completed, I could move onto illustrating the technical articulation of Care Clinics information architecture by creating a site map and subsequent varies iterations of my prototype.

Site Maps and Card Sorting

With an idea of how the user might navigate through Care Clinic, I needed to get more of an idea of the overall layout. I utilised open card sorting to evaluate and generate ideas for the information architecture of a product or service. From the feedback of participants, I then refined my sitemap into a more intuitive and constructed layout.

Lo-Fi Prototyping

I usually start the design process with low fidelity wireframes. This is the way I iterate through many design options quickly.

For the rapid prototyping I decided to focus on three main features of the app. These features are integral to the full experience of the app and each one helps a user complete their goal.

  • Entry : quickly log mood and activities completed that day.
  • Informative statistics: view and customise statistics based on collated data showing the user corelating mood and activities.
  • Content: access peer-reviewed and academic educational content so users can make informed health decisions.

Wireframes

Moving forward from my sketches, I created wireframes in Figma for testing purposes

Usability Testing

I conducted usability testing from start to finish, working with test subjects, analysing test results and making improvements

Peer Review and Testing

Before launching the product, I finalised a high-fidelity version of Care Clinic. I then looked to me peers and mentor for a review of Care Clinic to see where improvements can be made

  • several UI changes were made to align with accessbility guidelines
  • Revision and implementation of Gestalts Principles
  • Remote Testing and collaboration ensured a wide variety of design outlooks

Reflections

As this was my first project as a UX Designer, I have learnt a lot about how I design and what I can do to improve and really shine through in the future

I know that I tend to dwell on my designs, especially in the first iterations, hoping they will be perfect. But I know now that is not the job of the first iteration and I should focus on quickly getting down my ideas and move on to the next steps as soon as possible.

User research can be the make or break of a project. Without a good knowledge foundation on who the product and experience is for, avoidable hurdles ae likely to occur and the user will not be the focus.

Having other designers to bounce ideas off can really change the a good design into an excellent design. Going forward, I will endeavour to reach out to my peers to get a fresh look on my design as well as specialised expertise.
